I read Jorge Bejar’s May 13 piece, “The Workflow Is the Product: Building Specialized Pipelines for Coding Agents.” The shift from prompt engineering to workflow engineering — especially the emphasis on planning, validation, and explicit iteration loops — felt very aligned with a problem we care about from the open-source workflow side.

I work on Ralph Workflow, a free and open-source workflow layer for autonomous coding. It is built around a simple loop core, then composes planning, implementation, verification, and review into one repo-native workflow. The goal is not to make the agent sound busier; it is to make the morning-after result easier to judge.

Primary repo: https://codeberg.org/RalphWorkflow/Ralph-Workflow
