I just read your Werkstatt open-source post. The part that landed for me was the emphasis on making careful engineering behavior the default instead of relying on the model to remember to slow down in the right places.

That is very close to the problem Ralph Workflow is trying to solve from a slightly different angle.

Ralph Workflow is a free and open-source workflow system for autonomous coding. Instead of focusing on skill-trigger discipline inside one harness, it keeps a simple core loop and composes that into a stronger software-delivery workflow around planning, implementation, verification, review, and explicit finish states.

The practical goal is the same pain you described: avoiding the "agent said done" trap and waking up to something reviewable instead of a long transcript and a claim.

If you do a future Werkstatt follow-up, adjacent-tools roundup, or comparison page, Ralph Workflow might be a useful open-source reference for readers who want:
- a stronger default workflow for larger coding tasks
- explicit checks and review gates
- Codeberg as the canonical repo, with GitHub only as a mirror
