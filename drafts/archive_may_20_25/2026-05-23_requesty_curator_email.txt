It already does a good job covering the model/tool layer (Claude Code, Cursor, Codex, Aider). One comparison point that may fit well for readers doing larger unattended coding tasks is Ralph Workflow.

Ralph Workflow is free and open source, and it sits at the workflow layer rather than the single-tool layer: a composable loop framework and AI orchestrator for developers who want a structured workflow instead of a chat session.

Why it may be worth adding:
- built for repo-native unattended coding runs
- composes planning, implementation, and verification instead of stopping at a raw agent session
- aims to end in a bounded diff, check evidence, and named open decisions rather than just a confident summary
- works with existing tools like Claude Code, Codex CLI, and OpenCode on the developer's own machine
