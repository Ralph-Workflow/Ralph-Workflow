# RalphWorkflow daily marketing research — 2026-05-25

Scanned 33 candidate discussions across Reddit, Hacker News, and adjacent dev communities.

## Core themes
- Strong demand for unattended coding, but users distrust "run wild" autopilot.
- The winning language is orchestration, verification, worktree isolation, durable notes/specs, and reviewable evidence.
- Users repeatedly describe pain around context drift, merge conflicts, weak handoffs, and hosted/cloud privacy concerns.
- Multi-agent interest is real, but sentiment is skeptical unless the workflow has strong gates and human review.
- Mixed-model workflows (Claude + Codex + Gemini/OpenCode) are increasingly normal; vendor-neutral positioning matters.
