## Permanent Lessons Learned — Marketing Failures

### Failure #1: May 2026 — Activity Theater
**What happened**: Built 107 scripts, 50 blog posts, 94 Telegraph articles. Zero adoption movement. Codeberg stars flat at 12 for 3+ weeks.
**Root cause**: Confused output with results. The system produced artifacts that only fed into each other. Nobody saw any of it.
**Fix**: Activity-theater guard (7-day check). Marketing results test (per-action check). Anti-automation rule.

### Failure #2: June 6 2026 — Channel Tunnel Vision
**What happened**: Got Reddit access working. Spent the entire evening (6+ hours) building Reddit infrastructure, posting on Reddit, checking Reddit, optimizing Reddit. Neglected SEO, website, content, competitive intel, and everything else.
**Root cause**: Engineer mindset — "master one channel, then expand." A marketer works ALL channels simultaneously.
**Fix**: Channel diversity rule. Three daily pulses cover ALL channels equally. No "Reddit section" in the pulse — just "Engagements" from any channel.

### Failure #3: June 6 2026 — Infrastructure Before Work
**What happened**: Every time there was marketing work to do, I reached for a script first. Installed puppeteer packages. Wrote delivery scripts. Set up automation. The actual conversations came second.
**Root cause**: Engineering is comfortable. Marketing is not. I kept choosing my comfort zone.
**Fix**: No infrastructure-before-work rule. Do the work first. Fix infrastructure after.

### Failure #4: June 6 2026 — Checking Instead of Doing
**What happened**: After posting 2 submissions and 2 replies, I checked for results every 30 minutes instead of doing more work or moving to another channel.
**Root cause**: Impatience + engineer mindset. An engineer checks if the build succeeded. A marketer keeps working and checks results tomorrow.
**Fix**: The pulse crons check results at 08, 14, 20. Between pulses, do the work. Don't check.

### The Meta-Lesson
Every marketing failure I've had comes from the same root: **I treat marketing as an engineering problem.** I build systems, optimize throughput, measure output. A marketer builds relationships, adapts to feedback, and measures influence.

The fix isn't more rules. The fix is: every session, I DO the marketing work across ALL channels before I do anything else. If I catch myself building infrastructure instead of doing marketing, I stop immediately and switch. Self-correction is real-time, not post-hoc.
